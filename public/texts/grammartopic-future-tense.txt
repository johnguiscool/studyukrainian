<h3>Future Tense</h3>
<p>There are two ways to express action in the future in Ukrainian, through the future perfect tense, and the future imperfect tense.</p>
<p>The <strong>future perfect tense</strong> is used to express actions in such a way to emphasize that they will be completed (I will paint a painting, I will watch this movie).</p>
<p>The <strong>future imperfect tense</strong> is used to express that actions will be performed continuously (I will be reading a book, I will be visiting family).</p>
<h4><strong>Background</strong></h4>
<p>To understand how to form the <strong>future perfect tense</strong>, we have to describe how verbs work in Ukrainian. Verbs in Ukrainian can have two aspects: perfective and imperfective, distinguished by a prefix that varies from verb to verb. When a verb with the <strong>imperfective aspect</strong> is conjugated, the verb is in the <strong>present tense</strong>. When a verb with the <strong>perfective aspect</strong> is conjugated, the verb is in the <strong>future perfect tense</strong>.</p>

<h4><strong>Forming the future perfect tense</strong></h4>
<p>To form the <strong>future perfect tense</strong>:</p>
<p>(1) Take the imperfective form of the verb. (This is considered the base form) <strong>читати</strong></p>
<p>(2) Add a prefix to change it to the perfective aspect. <strong>про + читати = прочитати</strong></p>
<p>(3) Conjugate in the present tense based on the subject. <strong>Я прочитаю.</strong></p>
<p>The prefix that must be added to a verb to form the perfective form of the verb varies from verb to verb and must be memorized.</p>
