<h3 >
    The Genitive Case
</h3>
<p >
    <b>Formation of the Genitive Case</b>
</p>
<p >
    The genitive case is formed by modifying the ending of a noun. Although
    exceptions and special rules exist, these three simple rules are enough to
    cover how noun endings change in the vast majority of cases.
</p>
<ol>
    <li >
        <p >
            -а → -и
        </p>
    </li>
    <li >
        <p >
            -o → -а
            <br/>
            -е → -а
        </p>
    </li>
    <li >
        <p >
            [consonant] + a
        </p>
    </li>
</ol>

<p >
    <b>When to use the Genitive Case</b>
</p>
<ol>
    <li >
        <p >
            <b>To express origin / possession / ownership.</b>
            <br/>
            комісія - commission
            <br/>
            Звіт комісії - The commission’s report.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            собака - dog
            <br/>
            Батько собаки - The dog’s father
            <br/>
            <br/>
            Олег - Oleg
            <br/>
            Ноутбук Олега - Oleg’s notebook
            <br/>
            <br/>
        </p>
    </li>
    <li >
        <p >
            <b>Where the English word “of” would be used.</b>
            <br/>
            місяць - month
            <br/>
            кінець місяця - end of the month
            <br/>
            <br/>
        </p>
    </li>
    <li >
        <p >
            <b>With the adverbs з (from), до (to), від (away from).</b>
            <br/>
            Київ - Kiev, Львів - Lviv
            <br/>
            з Києва до Львова - from Kiev to Lviv*
            <br/>
            *Note that the vowels in the final syllable of a word can sometimes
            change in the genitive form.
        </p>
    </li>
</ol>
<ol start="4">
    <li >
        <p >
            <b>With the adverbs без (without).</b>
            <br/>
            молоко - milk
            <br/>
            кава без молока
            <br/>
            <br/>
        </p>
    </li>
</ol>
<p >
    <b>Tip on remembering how to use the genitive case:</b>
</p>
<p >
    So what does the word “genitive” even mean, and why is this one case used
    for so many different grammatical situations? The common theme behind all
    the different uses of the genitive case is one of either “origin” (the word
    genitive comes from the Latin word “genesis”), or of “negation / absence”
    (as with negative sentences and with the preposition без - “without”).
</p>
<p >
    <b>Genitive as expressing origin:</b>
</p>
<p >
    We can think of possession / belonging as expressing that the owner is the
    “origin” of an object. The verbs із and від both mean “from”, and indicate
    “being from” a place, or movement “away from” a place, again having this
    idea of “coming from” a point of origin. Even location words that use the
    genitive can be thought of as expressing location of something relative to
    a point of origin.
</p>
